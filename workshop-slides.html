THE AI-PROOF FRAMEWORK

3 questions.
If your offer passes all 3, AI can't touch you.

QUESTION 1 OF 3

Is this judgment or execution?

Execution = "Do this thing."
Judgment = "Decide which thing to do and why."

If a client can describe the output before you start — it's execution. AI will eat it.

EXECUTION

"Write 4 blog posts per month"

Client knows the output. You're the hands. AI is cheaper hands.

→
JUDGMENT

"Decide what's worth writing and why it'll convert"

Client doesn't know this. That's why they need you.

QUESTION 2 OF 3

Does this require context AI doesn't have?

AI knows the internet. It doesn't know your client's board dynamics,
their customer's real objections, or what happened last quarter.

The deeper your context, the harder you are to replace.

LOW CONTEXT

"Social media management"

Schedule posts, write captions, track metrics. AI + an intern.

→
HIGH CONTEXT

"Brand voice architect for a founder going through a pivot"

Requires understanding the person, the business, the timing.

QUESTION 3 OF 3

Would a client pay 3x if you disappeared?

If you vanished tomorrow, would your best client panic?
Or would they just find another vendor?

The panic test. If they'd panic, you're irreplaceable. If they'd shrug, you're a commodity.

REPLACEABLE

"SEO consultant — audits + reports"

Any competent SEO could do this. AI already does half of it.

→
IRREPLACEABLE

"The person who knows exactly which bet will move the needle this quarter"

They'd panic. Nobody else has your read on this business.

THE 3-QUESTION TEST

Your offer is AI-proof when:

  • It sells judgment, not execution
  • It requires context only you have
  • Clients would panic if you disappeared

EXISTING CLIENTS

How to introduce the repositioned offer.

  • Name the shift. "AI changed what execution is worth. I'm repositioning around what it can't do."
  • Show, don't pitch. Deliver one judgment-led insight before changing anything. Let them feel the difference.
  • Grandfather selectively. Best clients keep current pricing for 90 days. New clients pay new rates immediately.
  • Let some clients go. If they only want execution — they're not your client anymore. That's okay.
"I've been thinking about where I add the most value for you. It's not the deliverables — it's the decisions behind them. I'm restructuring my offer to focus there. Let me show you what that looks like." — Your next client conversation
