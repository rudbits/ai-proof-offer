Exercise 2: Find Your Uncomfortable Niche

2
The Uncomfortable Niche

The "uncomfortable niche" is a specific audience + specific outcome that makes you slightly uncomfortable. That's how you know it's right. Generic positioning gets lost. Specific, slightly scary positioning attracts.

"I help [SPECIFIC PERSON] achieve [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] — even though [THEIR DEEP FEAR]"

Why This Works

  • Specific → Clients self-select (no chasing)
  • Outcome-based → It's about their result, not your process
  • Fear-based → Taps into emotional motivation
  • Uncomfortable → If it doesn't scare you a little, it's not specific enough
Examples: Generic vs. Uncomfortable
Generic

"Help freelancers with marketing"

Uncomfortable Niche

"I help freelance designers who hate selling get 3 clients/month — without sending a single cold DM"

Generic

"Consulting for startups"

Uncomfortable Niche

"I help solo founders who've raised <$100k stop copying big-company strategies that don't work at their stage"

The AI-Proof Framework

These three questions are your filter for any offer, service, or product. Run everything through this lens.

Question 1
Where does my value sit — Execution, Strategy, or Judgment?

If Execution → Shift to Judgment. The higher up the stack, the more AI-proof you are.

Question 2
Is my niche specific and slightly uncomfortable?

If it doesn't make you slightly uncomfortable, it's not specific enough. Generic = invisible.

Question 3
What's my unique mechanism that AI can't replicate?

This is your unfair advantage. It could be taste, perspective, experience, or a proprietary framework.

✅ The AI-Proof Test: If you can answer all three questions with specificity and conviction, your offer is built to last. If any question makes you squirm, that's where the work is.
